  • Why is beatboxing important?

    Beatboxing not only teaches your child a greater sense of timing and rhythm, it also improves listening skills and can actually work to strengthen and protect his or her vocal cords. What's more, it will widen your child's musical scope, which will improve creativity and overall musicianship. [Source]

  • Why do you love beatboxing?

    It comes from you and only you. You establish your own style, you find your perfect timber, you possess all the skills you need to make the music that YOU want. It's the purest form of self expression. Just like how dancing has ballet, breaking, and contemporary styles, beatboxing has thousands of styles, too. [Source]

  • What is a beatbox called?

    Beatbox is also referred to as vocal percussion. Vocal percussion means imitating percussive sounds (including drum sounds) with the mouth. Traditionally, vocal percussionists in a cappella groups try to emulate real drum sounds. [Source]

  • Is beatboxing harmful?

    Beatboxers won't lose their voices anytime soon. ..Because beatboxing may annoy your neighbors, but it won't kill your vocal cords. Using your throat to create percussion does less damage to the vocal cords than singing does, according to University of Illinois researchers who published a study in the Journal of Voice. [Source]

  • Who invented the beatbox?

    However, as a form of art, human beatboxing could be traced back to the 1980s among the Hip-Hop circles. However, the first human beatbox was the self-proclaimed Doug E. Fresh, born on September 17, 1966. He was referred to as the “human beatbox”. [Source]

  • Is BeatBox an art?

    Beatboxing is a musical artform that uses the vocal tract to mimic percussion and other sound effects. .. Since then, beatboxing has become more widespread in mainstream culture, a cappella singing groups, and even as a stand-alone art form. [Source]
